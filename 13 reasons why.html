THiRTEEN R3ASON WHY

Thirteen Reasons Why is a young adult novel written by Jay Asher in 2007. The book tells the story of high school student Hannah Baker, who kills herself and leaves behind a box of 13 cassette tapes that detail the reasons for her suicide and call out the people she holds responsible. The novel is a first person narrative told from the perspective of Clay, a high school boy who receives 13 tapes that fellow student Hannah Baker leaves behind after her suicide. The book focuses on harassment and sexual assault.
